About us

Indian Creek Animal Shelter is independently owned and operated. We are a 501(c) non-profit that rescues abandoned animals and provides safe loving homes for them. During the course of their stay we make sure to provide food and safe shelter for the animals as well as veterinarian care. Your donations for adoptions help fund these causes as well as provide on site staffing. Please consider making one of our pets your fur-ever friend today!

Adoptable Dogs

Meet Max our Snow White sweetheart!

He’s part poodle/yorke mix and full of love. At the age of four he’s got plenty of spunk but also settles down to cuddle. Max came to us through a rescue mission from a litter of puppies found in an inhabitable situation. He gets along with the other animals here at the shelter and absolutely adores children. He also loves going for a long walk through the dog park. Max has also had all of his shots and is ready to take home today!

Our sad little bear.

A Pomeranian breed that has been feeling a little lonely since its owner dropped it off. Sadly bear doesn’t get a long with other animals but has an insatiable love for children. He also loves to cuddle and lay about on a lazy Sunday afternoon. Bear has been to the vet several times and is now up to date on all of his shots. He prefers being groomed with a soft brush to make sure that poof stays in tact.

Berniece the Bernard

Guaranteed this St. Bernard will be your best friend for many years to come. In fact, Benji is a ton of fun rolled into one. She has been fully trained to heel, sit, stay, play dead and shake paws! Of course she also loves to be rewarded with her favorite raw hide treats. Berniece came to us through a litter of puppies from a breeder that decided to no longer be in the business. All she needs now is a wonderful family to call her own.

Wyatt

This floppy eared German Shepherd has a fun sense of curiosity and a burst of energy to match. He was found alone wondering the west side which is why we named him Wyatt (after the famous Wild West frontiersman Wyatt Earp). He loves playing frisbee, ball, and is a big fan of swimming in the water! Wyatt is a little sensitive to other animals however but has no problem enjoying time with the kids or just a single friend. We made sure that he has all of his paperwork up to date and he can be yours for adoption today!

Star

Star is a year old full bred collie with a beautiful soft coat of cream and white fur. She is incredibly sweet and loves to lay by your feet and relax for hours while you read. Her favorite treats are biscuits and jerky sticks. Star is up to date on all of her shots and has even had all her preventative heart worm treatments. She would make an incredible pet for anyone.

Sam I am

Get ready to melt the hearts of people around you when you bring home this sweet ray of sunshine. Sam is absolutely adorable and can easily fit in his puppy travel bag to take on all sorts of vacations with you. He loves wearing cute little sweaters and outfits to parade around town. Also, he provides kisses galore! Sam is friendly with both kids and other animals.

Adoptable Cats

Dirty Snowball

This little kitten is full of life. He loves to play, climb and chase his friends around the center. His curious nature makes for an amusingly fun time. Snowball has been both neutered and declawed in the front. All of his vet papers are up to date making him perfect for apartment living. He was found in an abandoned warehouse and could really use a good loving place to call home.

Tortie

Our resident tortoise shell is a very independent soul and enjoys alone time perched in the window soaking up the sunlight. Her beautiful colors make her truly unique and set her apart from other feline breeds here at the shelter. Tortie also has a playful side and enjoys basking in catnip and batting around the occasional mouse. She has been declawed in the front, spayed, and is up to date on her shots.

Emerald

Distinctly named after her unique and beautiful eye color Emerald is the ideal pet for a cat lover. She loves any kind of attention and will take any chance she can get to cuddle up and purr. A big part of the attention is showing you her playful side and chasing string or feathers making for a joyful playtime together. Emerald is about 2 years old and is still full of love and life. Come on down to the shelter and take her home today!
